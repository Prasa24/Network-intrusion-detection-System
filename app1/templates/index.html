Intrusion Detection

Cloud intrusion refers to unauthorized access or malicious activities in cloud computing environments. These intrusions occur when attackers exploit vulnerabilities in the cloud infrastructure, applications, or user accounts to gain access to sensitive data, disrupt services, or manipulate systems. Cloud environments are often targeted due to their scalability and centralized storage, making them valuable repositories for data. Common methods of intrusion include phishing, exploiting misconfigured settings, weak passwords, or vulnerabilities in APIs. Preventing cloud intrusion requires robust security measures, such as encryption, multi-factor authentication (MFA), regular security audits, and real-time monitoring for unusual activity.

Impacts of Cloud Intrusion

Here are four impact in DOS Attack

  • Data Breach and Loss

    Cloud intrusions often lead to unauthorized access to sensitive or confidential information stored in the cloud. This can include personal data, financial records, trade secrets, or intellectual property.

  • Financial Loss

    Organizations may face significant financial repercussions due to downtime, loss of customer trust, or legal penalties resulting from compliance violations.

  • Service Disruption

    Intrusions can disrupt the normal operations of cloud-based services, leading to downtime or degraded performance.

  • Damage to Reputation

    A cloud intrusion can tarnish an organization's reputation by eroding customer trust and stakeholder confidence.

Types of Cloud Intrusion

Some of the common attacks are

  • Data Breaches

    Unauthorized access to sensitive information stored in the cloud, often due to weak security measures or misconfigurations.

  • Account Hijacking

    Attackers compromise user credentials to take control of cloud accounts and misuse resources or access data.

  • Insider Threats

    Malicious or negligent actions by employees or contractors who exploit their access to cloud systems.

  • Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

    Overloading cloud services with excessive requests, causing disruptions and making services unavailable to legitimate users.

  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

    Interception of data transmitted between users and the cloud, leading to data theft or manipulation.

  • API Exploitation

    Attacks that exploit vulnerabilities in cloud APIs to gain unauthorized access or control over cloud services.
